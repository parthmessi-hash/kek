MEET OUR TEAM

Kids Empower Kids is a youth-led movement dedicated to showing that kindness, leadership, and meaningful change can start at any age.

Aanya Remi

Aanya Remi is a senior at Saint Francis High School with a strong passion for youth leadership, community service, and creating meaningful impact. She is the founder of several youth-led initiatives, including Math Magicians Academy, a math enrichment club that helps elementary students strengthen problem-solving and competition math skills, and Mindful Me, a mindfulness and emotional wellness program designed to support children through engaging activities, journals, and guided lessons. Through her work, Aanya hopes to empower younger students with confidence, resilience, and opportunities to succeed both academically and personally.

In addition to her service initiatives, Aanya serves as a Board Member of TEDxSaintFrancisHS and the Student Moreau Board at Saint Francis. She is a two-time recipient of the Presidential Volunteer Service Award and a proud member of the National Honor Society. Aanya is also actively involved in band and debate, balancing her academic and extracurricular commitments with creativity, compassion, and leadership. She is deeply committed to using her voice and skills to inspire positive change in her community and beyond.

Pari Gupta

Pari Gupta is a sophomore at Cupertino High School with a passion for both the arts and athletics. A trained Kathak dancer, she brings grace, discipline, and dedication to every performance. She is also a competitive swimmer, skillfully balancing her commitment to the pool with her artistic pursuits.

Pari is an active member of her school’s Speech and Debate team, where she enjoys expressing ideas confidently and sharpening her critical thinking skills. She has a deep interest in coding and is currently working on an AI project, reflecting her curiosity and enthusiasm for technology.

In addition, Pari has been part of a Girl Scout troop for many years, where she enjoys leading, planning, and executing projects. She is especially passionate about community service and values opportunities to give back and make a positive impact.

Parth Gupta

Parth Gupta is an eighth grader at Stratford Middle School with a strong drive to learn and grow. He is a natural leader who enjoys taking initiative and leading both school and outside-of-school team projects.

Parth is the captain of his competitive soccer team, bringing energy, discipline, and teamwork to every game while motivating his teammates to perform their best. He also enjoys public speaking and confidently shares his ideas, often inspiring those around him.

Beyond academics and sports, Parth has a wide range of interests. He enjoys coding, cooking, and playing chess, activities that reflect his creativity, problem-solving skills, and curiosity.

Nitya Remi

Nitya Remi is a 5th grader at Monticello Academy who has been actively involved in community service since the age of six. She brings enthusiasm, creativity, and kindness to every project she participates in and loves helping make others feel supported and valued. Nitya believes that giving back to the community can be both meaningful and fun, and she enjoys finding ways to spread positivity through service.

In addition to volunteering, Nitya participates in Speech and Debate, where she continues to build her confidence, public speaking, and communication skills. She also enjoys swimming and expressing her creativity through music by playing both the piano and saxophone. With her positive attitude, caring spirit, and excitement for helping others, Nitya is passionate about making a difference in her community in her own unique way.
